Bossa Nova Robotics | 2019-2020

Operations Efficiency

Founded the Data Health team and built an investigative framework that drove 25% improvement in core customer metrics and doubled technician fleet capacity.

Context

Bossa Nova Robotics deployed AI-powered inventory robots in major retail stores. The robots autonomously navigated store aisles, scanning shelves to identify out-of-stock products and misplaced inventory. The product promise was simple: give retailers real-time visibility into what's on their shelves. The operational reality was much messier.

Problem

Unknown operational failures were degrading customer-facing metrics, and nobody had a systematic way to find them. Robot telemetry data, sensor readings, and customer outcome data all lived in different places. When a robot failed a scan or produced bad data, it wasn't clear whether the root cause was hardware, software, environmental, or operational. Issues surfaced as customer complaints, not as proactive diagnostics.

The company was scaling its fleet without understanding why some robots and some stores performed significantly worse than others.

Approach

  • Founded the Data Health team. This was a cross-functional team I created and led. Its purpose was to surface unknown operational failures before customers felt them. No one had owned this problem before.
  • Built an investigative framework. Combined robot telemetry, sensor data, and customer outcome data to diagnose root causes of performance variation. This framework turned vague "something's wrong" signals into specific, actionable findings.
  • Uncovered hidden correlations. Analyzed telemetry patterns and found specific failure modes that correlated directly with customer-facing outcomes like uptime and out-of-stock identification accuracy. These weren't obvious from any single data source.
  • Redirected the product roadmap. Used findings to shift roadmap priorities toward data capture and autonomy improvements. This was a significant change: the team had been focused on feature expansion, but the data showed that reliability and data quality had more impact on customer metrics.
  • Scaled operations without headcount. Analyzed case management data and implemented new routing systems that doubled technician capacity from 24 to 48 robots per technician.
